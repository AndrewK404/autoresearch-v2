# 005 — Catalog of cycles with shortcut length L >= 4

Companion script: `autoresearch/archive/005-cataloger.py`. Source: `autoresearch/archive/001-cycles.parquet` (153 cycles across 44 (a, b) variants).

## Section 1 — Setup

Of the 153 cycles in the atlas, **116** have shortcut length L <= 3 and **37** have L >= 4. The L <= 3 cycles were enumerated in closed form by action 002 because the shortcut-step equation

    n0 * (2^K - a^L) = b * sum_{i=0}^{L-1} a^{L-1-i} * 2^{k_1+...+k_i}

is small enough at L = 1, 2, 3 to brute-force over the halving exponents k_i. At L >= 4 the search space explodes (eight or more free integer parameters once we include k_4, k_5, ...), so the L >= 4 atlas is recovered only by direct simulation in action 001's sweep. This file catalogs that residual set.

## Section 6 — Patterns observed

**L vs t_period.** Across the 37 L >= 4 cycles, t_period / L ranges from 2.50 to 4.00 (mean 3.08). For T_{a,b} with a = 3 the heuristic prediction is t_period / L ~ 1 + log_2(3) ~ 2.585; for a = 5 it is 1 + log_2(5) ~ 3.32; for a = 7 it is 1 + log_2(7) ~ 3.81. Per-a breakdown:

| a | n_L4plus | mean t_period/L | predicted 1 + log2(a) |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 1 | 6 | 2.917 | 1.000 |
| 3 | 15 | 2.655 | 2.585 |
| 5 | 12 | 3.417 | 3.322 |
| 7 | 4 | 3.867 | 3.807 |

**gcd inheritance.** For each L >= 4 cycle we record the gcd of its members (which by action 004 equals the inherited scale). Cycles with gcd = 1 are primitive in (a, b); cycles with gcd = d > 1 are scalings of a (a, b/d) cycle by d.

| gcd of members | n_cycles |
|---:|---:|
| 1 | 32 |
| 3 | 5 |

**No L >= 4 cycle spans multiple gcd classes** (each cycle's members share a common gcd by construction — T_{a,b} preserves gcd(n, b) when gcd(a, ...) is coprime, so this is expected and consistent with action 004's inheritance tagging).
